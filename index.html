The Mortality Atlas

Exploring 30 Years of Death Around the Globe

0.5 seconds.

That is roughly the interval at which someone dies somewhere in the world.

That equals two deaths every second,
over 170,000 deaths each day,
and nearly 60 million deaths every year.

These numbers are not random. They reflect patterns shaped by geography, time, and cause.

Some deaths decline as healthcare improves. Others persist where resources never arrive. Many are preventable.

What do people die from? Where do mortality rates fall, and where do they remain high? And most importantly: where would intervention save the most lives?
